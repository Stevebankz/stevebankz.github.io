Research Interests

Explainable & Robust AI

Layer-wise interpretability (LRP), causal rationale stability, and robust evaluation under distribution shift and adversarial perturbations.

Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning

Bridging neural representations with symbolic constraints and causal abstractions to verify and align model reasoning with its actions.

Social Harm Detection

Interpretable NLP for misinformation and hate speech detection, emphasizing fairness, reliability, and mitigation of harmful bias.

Multimodal Interpretability

Extending XAI methods from text to vision systems for real-world monitoring, welfare, environmental, and safety applications.

Current Research Directions

  • • Robust Explainability: Studying stability and faithfulness of explanations under distribution shift and adversarial perturbations.
  • • Causal & Neuro-Symbolic AI: Integrating symbolic constraints and causal abstractions into neural pipelines for verifiable reasoning.
  • • Social Harm & Misinformation: Building interpretable multilingual NLP systems for hate speech and disinformation detection.
